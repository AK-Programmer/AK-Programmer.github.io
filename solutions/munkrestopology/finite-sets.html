Problem 2: Show that if \(B\) is not finite and \(B \subset A\), then \(A\) is not finite.

Given \(X = \{0, 1\}\), this problem is asking us to find a bijective correspondence between \(X^{\omega}\) and a proper subset of \(X^{\omega}\). Remembering that \(X^{\omega}\) is the set of all \(\omega\)-tuples \((\omega_1, \omega_2, \ldots)\) such that \(\omega_{i} \in X \forall i\), let us define a proper subset of \(X\), \(X - \{x_o\}\) where \(x_{o} \in X\).
