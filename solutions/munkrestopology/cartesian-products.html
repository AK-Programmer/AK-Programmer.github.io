Munkres - Cartesian Products

Adar Kahiri
June 2020

Problem 1: Show there is a bijective correspondence of \(A \times B\) with \(B \times A\)

Since \((a, b) \in A \times B \iff (b, a) \in B \times A\), we can map any element \((a, b)\) to the element \((b, a)\) and vice versa, and so there is clearly a bijective correspondence between the two sets.


Problem 3: Let \(A = A_1 \times A_2 \times \cdots\) and \(B = B_1 \times B_2 \times \cdots\)
  • (a) Show that if \(B_i \subset A_i\) for all \(i\), then \(B \subset A\)
  • (b) Show the converse of (a) holds if \(B\) is nonempty.
  • (c) Show that if \(A\) is nonempty, each \(A_i\) is nonempty. Does the converse hold?
  • (d) What is the relation between the set \(A \cup B\) and the cartesian product \[\prod\limits_{i \in \mathbb{Z}_{+}} A_{i} \cup B_{i}.\] What is the relation between the set \(A \cap B\) and the cartesian product \[\prod\limits_{i \in \mathbb{Z}_{+}} A_{i} \cap B_{i}.\]
(a) The first part of this question is asking to show that given \(A = A_1 \times A_2 \times \cdots\) and \(B = B_1 \times B_2 \times \cdots\), if \(B_{i} \subset A_{i}\), then \(B \subset A\). Remembering that \[\prod\limits_{i \in \mathbb{Z}_{+}} S_{i} = S_1 \times S_2 \times \cdots\] is defined as the set of all \(\omega\)-tuples \((\omega_1, \omega_2, \cdots)\) of elements of \(S_1 \cup S_2 \cup \cdots\) such that \(\omega_1 \in S_1, \omega_2 \in S_2, \cdots\), it follows that for any given \(\omega_{i} \in B_{i}\), \(\omega_{i}\) must also be in \(A_{i}\), and so any \((\omega_1, \omega_2, \cdots) \in B\) must also be in \(A\).
